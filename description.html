Artist Statement

I draw things from real life, without the drawings themselves being completely realistic. The objects are in conversation with surrounding metaphors and figures, building towards compositions that are both legible and evocative. Using imagery, the structures that are tightly involved with creating perception and possible imaginations are loosened, reverting back to the simplicity of things that are already in place. ie, the body, the objects, the spaces and the conversations carried out between ourselves and these outer things. The process of creating these landscapes - observing closely yet never resolutely naming - harbours spontaneity, intuitiveness, freedom and focus. To be looked over in an honest way. Decoding and realising emotional streams running through.
